About

I am a Microeconomist and applied econometrician with research interests in Taxation, Public Economics, and Decision Theory.

My current research agenda focuses on the effects of corporate and dividend taxation on firms, the effect of corporate tax avoidance behaviour on the measurement of economic inequality, public economics, and the effects of uncertainty on entrepreneurship, pollution, and taxes. I am currently working on multiple projects investigating these issues in Canada, and Chile. These projects use a combination of applied economic theory, development of new empirical methods and the use of administrative datasets to provide rigorous answers to the questions that I aim to tackle. For all of them, I bring together my thorough knowledge of public finance, applied theory, applied econometrics, and vast experience working with large- scale administrative datasets, and statistical tools.

Working Papers

• "Decentralizing the Chilean Miracle: Regional Intergenerational Mobility in a Developing Country"
with Javier Cortés Orihuela, Juan D. Díaz, Alexis Montecinos, Pablo A. Troncoso, and Gabriel I. Villarroel
Revise & Resubmit at Regional Studies .
      Abstract

We estimate spatially disaggregated measures of intergenerational mobility in Chile through an administrative dataset linking child’s and their parent’s earnings from the formal private labour sector. We report remarkable heterogeneity as we find higher and lower upward mobility in mining and agricultural regions, respectively, corroborating Connolly et al. (2019) with the distinction that Chile is a unitary form of government, implying that factors other than institutional differences shape mobility. We also find statistically significant gender gaps in persistence in poverty, persistence in privilege, and upward mobility.
